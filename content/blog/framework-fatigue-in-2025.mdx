**Framework fatigue** is real — and in 2025, it’s hitting harder than ever. With options like **Next.js**, **Remix**, **Astro**, and **Qwik**, developers face a constant dilemma: which stack is right, and which one will be outdated by next quarter?

## What’s Causing the Fatigue?

The explosion of **JavaScript frameworks**, build tools, and rendering strategies has made the dev landscape more powerful — but also more confusing. Every tool promises speed, DX, and SEO, but **not all stacks are built for your needs**.

> “The biggest problem in frontend isn’t speed — it’s stability.”  
> — [Lea Verou](https://twitter.com/LeaVerou/status/1718235670021386591)

## Choosing the Right Stack (Fast)

- **For SEO-heavy sites**: [**Astro**](https://astro.build/) or [**Next.js**](https://nextjs.org/)
- **For real-time apps**: [**Remix**](https://remix.run/) or **React Server Components**
- **For speed & simplicity**: **HTMX** or [**SvelteKit**](https://kit.svelte.dev/)

Stick to tools with **strong docs**, **stable roadmaps**, and an **active community**. The goal? Ship without getting stuck.

## Final Word

Don’t pick what’s hot — pick what’s **proven and fits your use case**. In 2025, the smartest devs aren’t chasing trends; they’re choosing **boring, reliable tech** that just works.
